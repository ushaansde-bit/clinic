FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about physiotherapy answered by Dr. Aarthi Ganesh

How can I relieve back pain without surgery?

Back pain can often be relieved without surgery through physiotherapy treatments including fascial manipulation, core strengthening exercises, posture correction, and manual therapy. At Shree Physiotherapy Clinic, our Italy-certified Fascial Manipulation technique provides immediate pain relief. Most patients see significant improvement in 3-5 sessions.

What is the best treatment for neck pain?

The best treatment for neck pain includes physiotherapy with manual therapy, neck strengthening exercises, ergonomic corrections, and fascial manipulation. Avoiding prolonged screen time, maintaining proper posture, and regular stretching are key. Dr. Aarthi Ganesh specializes in treating cervical spondylosis and tech neck.

Do you provide home physiotherapy in Coimbatore?

Yes, we provide home physiotherapy services in Periyanaickenpalayam, Karamadai, and surrounding areas of Coimbatore. Dr. Aarthi Ganesh offers home visits for elderly patients, post-surgery rehabilitation, and those unable to visit the clinic. Call 9092294466 to book.

How many sessions are needed for frozen shoulder?

Frozen shoulder typically requires 10-15 physiotherapy sessions over 6-12 weeks. Treatment includes fascial manipulation, stretching exercises, and progressive mobilization. With proper treatment, most patients regain full shoulder mobility. Early intervention leads to faster recovery.

Can physiotherapy cure sciatica?

Yes, 90% of sciatica cases can be cured without surgery through physiotherapy. Treatment includes nerve gliding exercises, core stabilization, fascial release, and posture correction. Our specialized approach addresses the root cause of sciatic nerve compression for lasting relief.

Is physiotherapy effective for knee arthritis?

Yes, physiotherapy is highly effective for knee arthritis. Treatment includes strengthening exercises for quadriceps and hamstrings, balance training, manual therapy, and pain management. Physiotherapy can delay or prevent knee replacement surgery in many cases.
